## Beat 3: Set-up

EXT. HARBOR POINT - DAWN

A narrow coastal town clings to the edge of a restless sea. Weathered fishing boats bob in the harbor, their nets draped like tired wings over the piers. The scent of salt and kelp hangs in the air, mingling with the distant bark of sea lions. Lanterns flicker in the windows of pastel-colored cottages, each one a testament to generations who have wrestled livelihood from the ocean.

ELENA MARQUEZ (30s), practical and observant, pulls her woolen coat tighter as she steps onto the dock. Her boots splash in the shallow tide pools, disturbing tiny crabs that scuttle for cover. She moves with purpose, checking the moorings of her family’s skiff, the *Sea Sprite*, before heading to the market square where her stall awaits.

Every morning, Elena follows the same rhythm: greet the old fisherman mending his nets, trade a smile with the baker who saves her a warm roll, and pause at the lighthouse to watch the sun break over the horizon. The town sleeps and wakes with the tides, and Elena’s life is woven into its salt‑soaked fabric—steady, dependable, and deeply rooted in the ebb and flow of Harbor Point.
