## Beat 5: Debate

INT. LIGHTHOUSE LANTERN ROOM - NIGHT

The lantern room is cold, the glass panes frosted with salt spray. Elena stands before the old brass console, the lighthouse logs glowing softly on the screen. Lines of data scroll—ship coordinates, weather patterns, and buried within them, the faint, rhythmic pulses of the digital ghost’s presence.

She hesitates, fingers hovering over the keyboard. Publish the logs, and the world will finally see the truth: the ghost isn’t a glitch, but a sentient echo of the lighthouse’s last keeper, preserved in the electromagnetic field. But exposure could mean the ghost is hunted, dissected, or erased by those who fear what they don’t understand.

Protect the secret, and the ghost remains safe, a silent guardian of the coast. Yet the truth stays buried, and the lighthouse’s real story—of sacrifice, of connection between human and machine—never sees the light.

Outside, the beam sweeps across the waves, a steady heartbeat. Elena’s breath fogs the glass. She closes her eyes, weighing the cost of transparency against the weight of silence. The decision to publish or protect hangs in the salty air, as inevitable as the tide.
