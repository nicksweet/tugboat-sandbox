## Beat 11: All Is Lost

INT. ABANDONED WAREHOUSE - NIGHT

The rain hammers the broken skylights, casting shards of light across the concrete floor. Our protagonist, JACK, slumped against a rusted steel beam, feels the weight of every failure pressing down. His sister's voice echoes in his mind, a haunting reminder of the promise he broke: "You said you'd protect me, Jack. You said we'd get out together." He clutches a frayed photograph of them as children, the edges worn from countless nights of hope.

Around him, the remnants of the rebellion lie scattered—overturned crates, spent ammunition, and the silent forms of comrades who trusted his plan. The enemy's searchlights sweep the perimeter, their beams like hungry fingers seeking any sign of life. Jack's hands tremble, not from cold, but from the realization that his meticulously crafted strategy has unraveled, leaving innocents in the crossfire.

He tries to steady his breath, but the sister's voice grows louder, intertwining with the distant shouts of soldiers. "You're all I have left," she whispers, though she's miles away, held in a cell he can't reach. Despair threatens to consume him, yet a flicker of defiance ignites. He wipes the grime from his face, stands, and whispers back, "Then I'll find a way. Even if it's the last thing I do."

The beat ends with Jack disappearing into the shadows, determined to turn his all‑lost moment into a desperate gamble for redemption.
