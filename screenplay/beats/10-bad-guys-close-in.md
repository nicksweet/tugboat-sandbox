## Beat 10: Bad Guys Close In

INT. PLATFORM COUNSEL OFFICE - DAY

The platform counsel, a sharp-eyed attorney with a reputation for navigating treacherous regulatory waters, sits across from the protagonist. Stacks of compliance reports and legal briefs clutter the desk, each page a potential landmine. Outside, the city hums with the restless energy of adversaries gathering—bad guys closing in, their motives obscured by layers of corporate veil and whispered threats.

The counsel leans forward, voice low and urgent. "They're not just after your code; they're after the trust users place in the platform. If we don't tighten the access controls and audit trails now, the fallout could sink us all." A flicker of resolve crosses the protagonist's face as they grasp the stakes: the bad guys aren't just external hackers but also internal pressures seeking to exploit any weakness.

Outside the window, dark clouds gather, mirroring the gathering storm. The counsel slides a redacted document across the table—a warning shot from a rival firm. "Consider this your last chance to act before they move." The protagonist nods, the weight of impending conflict settling like a promise. The scene tightens as the counsel rises, preparing to marshal legal defenses while the protagonist readies for the inevitable confrontation, knowing the battle lines are drawn and the bad guys are already at the gate.
