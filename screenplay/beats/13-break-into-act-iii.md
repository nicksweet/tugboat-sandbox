## Beat 13: Break into Act III

INT. LABORATORY - NIGHT

The room hums with the low thrum of machines. Papers scatter across the desk, each covered in frantic equations and half-formed hypotheses. DR. LENA VARGAS, eyes bloodshot but blazing, stares at the readout on the screen. A single number pulses—impossible, yet undeniable.

She leans back, breath catching. The data confirms the anomaly isn’t noise; it’s a signal, a pattern repeating every 3.7 seconds, synchronized with the tidal fluctuations they’ve been monitoring for weeks. Her mind races, connecting dots she’d previously dismissed as coincidence.

“Eureka,” she whispers, the word barely audible over the whir of the centrifuges. It’s not just a cry of triumph; it’s the moment the fractured pieces of her theory snap into place. The breakthrough she’s chased for months finally materializes, offering a clear path through the impasse that has stalled the project.

Outside, the storm intensifies, rain lashing the windows like impatient fingers. Inside, Lena feels a surge of clarity. She grabs her notebook, sketching a rough diagram of the feedback loop that could amplify the signal enough to breach the barrier they’ve been trying to penetrate. The realization hits: they don’t need more power; they need precision.

She turns to her colleague, MALIK, who’s been monitoring the auxiliary feeds. “We’ve got it,” she says, voice steady despite the adrenaline. “Now we break into Act III.”

They exchange a look—part relief, part fierce determination—as the laboratory lights flicker, heralding the next phase of their daring experiment.
