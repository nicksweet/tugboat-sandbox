## Beat 2: Theme Stated

INT. LIGHTHOUSE INTERIOR - NIGHT

The massive Fresnel lens rotates slowly, casting its familiar beam across the turbulent sea. Yet the lighthouse interior has changed: brass instruments and oil lamps have been replaced by racks of humming servers, fiber-optic cables snaking along the stone walls, and holographic readouts floating in the salt-air mist.

KEEPER (40s), eyes tired from weeks of solitary watch, monitors the digital console. A sudden anomaly flickers on the screen—a translucent figure weaving through the data streams, composed of shifting glyphs and faint sonar pings. It’s the DIGITAL GHOST, an echo of the lighthouse’s original keepers, now encoded in the system’s forgotten backups and weather-log archives.

The ghost doesn’t speak; it pulses in rhythmic patterns that match the lighthouse’s sweep. As the beam cuts through the fog, the ghost’s form brightens, then fades, leaving behind a single line of text on the console: “WE KEPT THE LIGHT.”

Keeper leans back, realizing the theme staring back from the code: no matter how automated the beacon becomes, its soul remains the human promise to guide the lost home—a promise that haunts the machine like a ghost in the machine.
